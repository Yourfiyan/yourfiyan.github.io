date: "March 2026"

It all started when I was 14. A curiosity about how websites work led me to open Notepad and type my first <html> tag. That simple act of creating something from nothing—a heading that appeared in a browser—was all it took to get me hooked.

The early days were filled with experiments: colorful pages with questionable design choices, buttons that didn't do anything, and lots of copy-pasting from tutorials. But every broken layout and every console error taught me something new.

JavaScript was my first real challenge. Moving from static pages to interactive ones felt like unlocking a superpower. I built a calculator (Calculato Ready) and a music player—simple projects, but each one pushed my skills further.

Then came Python, which opened up a whole new world beyond the browser. Scripts that automated tasks, programs that solved math problems, and eventually AI-powered applications. Linux became my playground for experimentation.

Today, I'm building multi-agent AI systems, managing inventory apps with PHP and MySQL, and continuously exploring new technologies. The journey from that first HTML tag to here has been incredible, and I know the best is yet to come.

If you're just starting out: embrace the struggle. Every error message is a lesson. Every project, no matter how small, is a step forward. Keep building, keep learning, and don't be afraid to experiment.
